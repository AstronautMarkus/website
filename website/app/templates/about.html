Fourth Computer, Second Upgrade Gaming PC

My first upgrade applied to my gaming PC, an upgrade I made in 2021 during quarantine that allowed me to enjoy more recent and demanding games. It was a process of learning and experimentation, but it was worth it for the improved performance and the ability to play more modern titles.

Two 8GB DDR4 RAM modules at 3200 MHz were added, which significantly improved performance in games and multitasking. Additionally, the 128GB SSD was replaced with a 512GB NVMe, another 1TB SSD for games, and a 3TB HDD for mass storage. For cooling, a 120mm AIO liquid cooler was added, which allowed me to maintain lower temperatures during long gaming sessions. I also upgraded to the AMD Ryzen 5 3600X processor for a significant performance boost. Overall, this upgrade was a great step forward in terms of performance and storage capacity, and it allowed me to enjoy a much smoother and more satisfying gaming experience. Finally, I upgraded to the GTX 1650, which wasn't a brutal change but the improvement in more recent and demanding games was noticeable.
